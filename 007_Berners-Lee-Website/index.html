Education

He attended Sheen Mount Primary School, and then went on to attend south-west London's Emanuel School from 1969 to
1973. He studied at The Queen's College, Oxford, from 1973 to 1976, where he received a first-class Bachelor of Arts degree
in physics. While at university, Berners-Lee made a computer out of an old television set, which he bought from a repair shop.

The Internet

In 984, he returned to Cern as a fellow. In 1989, CERN was the largest Internet node in Europe and Berners-Lee saw an
opportunity to join hypertext with the Internet: 'l just had to take the hypertext idea and connect it to the TCP and DNS ideas
and—ta-da!—the World Wide Web'. In 1990 he created not only the first web browser but also an editor and the first web
server. The first website. an explanation of the WWW concept, was published on 1990-12-20 • available to the internet Via the
CERN network.
